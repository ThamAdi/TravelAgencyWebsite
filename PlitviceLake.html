Plitvice Lake,Croatia

Located about halfway between the Croatian capital Zagreb & Zadar on the coast of the Adriatic sea, Plitvice Lakes are a magical world of living, moving water surrounded by

ancient forests, 16 lakes linked by waterfalls, bridges natural & man-made, & 300square kilometres of eild beauty full of bears, wolves, boars & birds.



With its exceptional natural beauty, this area has always attracted nature lovers, & already on 8th April 1949, it was proclaimed Croatia's 1st national park.The process of tufa

formation, which results in the building of tufa,or travertine, barriers & resulted in the creation of lakes, is the outstanding universal value, for which the Plitvice Lakes were

internationally recognized on 26th October ,1979 with their inscription onto the UNESCO World Heritage List.





Well known for its system of cascading lakes whereby 16 lovely lakes are interconnected by cascades &

waterfalls. The park is simply beautiful,the main focus of park's beauty are 16 lakes divided into 2 parts-

Upper Lakes & Lower Lakes,as well as numerous waterfalls & cascades.









The park is also populated by variety of animals including bears, wolves, otters, wild boars,

skunks, squirrels, rabbits, foxes & numerous bird species.

The country's largest National Park is a cascading series of waterfalls, caves & pools that

are homes to an enormous wildlife population.While summer is the most popular time to

visit, the FROZEN CATARACTS are equally stunning in WINTER.
